# Gear, Economy, and Lifestyle

---

## Price Categories (p. 385)

All items fall into one of eight **Price Categories**, which determine their **Market Price**.

| Price Category | Market Price |
|---|---|
| Cheap | 10eb |
| Everyday | 20eb |
| Costly | 50eb |
| Premium | 100eb |
| Expensive | 500eb |
| Very Expensive | 1,000eb |
| Luxury | 5,000eb |
| Super Luxury | 10,000eb and up |

**Without** the help of an experienced **Fixer** or one of their **Night Markets**, only items up to **Premium** can be purchased. If it's **Expensive** or above and you don't know a Fixer, you can't get it.

## Making Money (pp. 381-386)

### Doing Jobs (p. 381)

Pay is roughly equal to the level of mortal danger involved.

| Job Type | Pay (Per Person) | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Easy Job | 500eb | Armed resistance not expected |
| Typical Job | 1,000eb | Armed resistance expected, can prepare |
| Dangerous Job | 2,000eb | Overwhelming armed resistance; without heavy prep, you die |

When you have a full **7 days** free, you can hustle to earn eb. Roll **1d6** and consult your Role's Hustle table. Pay scales with **Role Ability Rank**:

## Lifestyle (p. 377)

Your **Lifestyle** determines how you eat, what entertainment you can afford, and your general quality of life. Paid monthly. If you don't pay within one week of the start of a month, you roll a **Death Save** at the start of each day until you do.

| Lifestyle | Monthly Cost | What It Entails |
|---|---|---|
| **Kibble** | 100eb | Horrible food. One movie or braindance per month. |
| **Generic Prepak** | 300eb | Decent food. Can go out to a good bar/restaurant each weekend. |
| **Good Prepak** | 600eb | Restaurant-quality food. Excellent bars/restaurants. One live concert or sporting event per month. |
| **Fresh Food** | 1,500eb | Real food. Hotel room once/month if needed. Executive bar access. One world-class restaurant meal per month. |

### Sleep Rules (p. 377)

- Going without **6 hours of sleep** per day gives **-2 to everything** for each day since last full sleep.
- **Sleeping uncomfortably** (crammed housing, street without passing DV15 Endurance, wilderness without DV15 Wilderness Survival) makes you **fatigued** the next day: **-2 to everything** while fatigued.
- **Crammed** = more persons than 1 + number of bedrooms. Cube Hotels are always uncomfortable with 2+ people.

### Housing At Character Creation (p. 105)

- **Non-Exec characters** start in a rented **Cargo Container** (Suburbs or Combat Zone) with a **Kibble Lifestyle**. First month's rent and kibble are free. Owe **1,100eb** on the 1st of next month.
- **Execs** start in a **Corporate Conapt** (Corporate Zone) with a **Good Prepak Lifestyle** (600eb/month), rent-free.
- **Nomads** have additional Housing options via their **Moto** Role Ability (see `roles-and-abilities.md`).

## Starting Gear by Method (pp. 98-105)

### Streetrats & Edgerunners (p. 98)

Weapons, armor, gear, and outfit are **predetermined** by Role. In addition, all Streetrat and Edgerunner characters get **500eb** to spend on other items or save.

### Complete Package Characters (p. 104)

- **2,550eb** to buy any weapons, armor, gear, and cyberware.
- **Additional 800eb** to spend only on **Fashion** and **Fashionware** (unspent amount is lost).
